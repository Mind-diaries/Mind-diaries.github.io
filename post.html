Jenga!

Strip away the fear, stack up your love

Posted by Sukanya Goswami on August 06, 2021

“Hey!”, a cord strikes when we see this pop on our notification and our heartbeat does a little hop and then we slide it away in lieu of not wanting to look desperate. But we’re desperate. Not because we’re miserable souls wandering lustfully for the mere illusion of a physical touch. Because the idea of attraction, its persuasion and perhaps the resulting exchange of bodily fluids (if you’re lucky) is a taboo in our country. So, what do we do? We take it as a challenge. We take up courses in the farthest city from home, announce meet-ups with imaginary friends, extend hypothetical class hours, twist our schedules to earn some extra bucks, for let’s admit—we have some relevance of self-respect.

The overloading thrill is no less than catching a train, that’s about to leave the station, just that the train here bounces and decelerates just when we’re trying to jump on. And if the person of interest happens to reside in a different geography, demography or social structure vis-à-vis a different city or state then we better shockproof our mental and emotional case because there’s no yardstick that can measure the trauma and turmoil for this commute. All this while juggling our work, studies, societal pressures. One wrong pull and our life’s Jenga. If we ever happen to write a guidebook, the title will definitely read ‘101 ways: scoring a paid internship is easier than dating in India’.

Let’s get to the meatier part

Who’s to blame? We aren’t the only generation that has ever wanted to venture into the ‘Garden of Love’. Euphonious songs constituting the preamble of desires ‘Kabhi Kabhi Dil Mein Khayal Aata Hai’, mom’s handwritten letters inside dad’s diary, ballads and poems dating back millennia are testaments of the heart’s desire. So, why does a country as young as it is, not embrace the playfulness of a love affair? Why do we need to fight the doctrines and commandments of such futile matters? Are we not courageous enough to lay out the syntax of the modern-day fling or are the liaisons too bold to cast aside? For all we know, the skillful management of this dalliance of sorts is a delight and a burden coincidentally.

But wait!

This façade doesn’t just end here. It takes a full 180 if the person for whom we took the trouble of living at the knife’s edge, convincing ourselves that there’s a rainbow at the end of tunnel ends up…cheating. Now begins the Holier-Than-thou journey to solace.
